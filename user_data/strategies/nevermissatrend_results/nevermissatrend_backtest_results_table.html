Equity Curve Plot

This performance chart tracks results over a certain period, illustrating the success of an activity or project by showing periodic gains or losses and the total outcome over time.
  • The X-axis (Time) represents the passage of time, with markers indicating specific intervals for when data was collected or recorded.
  • The Y-axis (Value) displays metrics such as profits, costs, or other results. It quantifies the outcome for each period and cumulatively.
  • Bars represent values for each time interval. Upward bars show positive results (gains or growth), while downward bars represent negative values (losses or declines).
  • The Cumulative Line aggregates the values over time, showing the overall trend and total value accrued from the start.
  • Special Markers, like dashed or dotted vertical lines, highlight significant dates or events for reference and provide context for changes in performance.
Interpretation:
  • Reading the Data: Start from the earliest time point and move forward chronologically. Observe individual bars for short-term performance, and follow the cumulative line to understand the overall trend and total result.
  • Identifying Patterns: Look for consistent patterns, such as a series of gains or losses. Noticing how the cumulative line moves can reveal stability, volatility, or trends over time.
  • Interpreting the Outcome: If the cumulative line ends higher than it started, the net result is positive. If it ends lower, it indicates a negative outcome.

This chart provides an overview of performance trends, helping users understand both short-term results and long-term outcomes.

Profitability Metrics

Metrics evaluating the profitability of the strategy, including profit percentage, win percentage, profit factor, and pairs with profit percentage.
  • Profit Percentage: Indicates the percentage of trading periods ending in profit. Higher values suggest consistent profitability over time.
  • Win Percentage: Measures the proportion of profitable trades. A high win rate often reflects strong trade execution and strategy robustness.
  • Profit Factor: Calculates the ratio of gross profits to gross losses. Values above 1 indicate profitability, while higher values indicate better risk-reward trade-offs.
  • Pairs with Profit Percentage: Shows the percentage of profitable trading pairs. Useful for understanding performance diversification across multiple instruments.

Key Takeaway: High profitability metrics suggest the strategy generates consistent and diversified returns across multiple instruments or trading periods.

Risk Metrics

Metrics assessing the risk exposure of the strategy, including drawdown and ES_CVaR.
  • Drawdown: The largest single drop from peak to trough in portfolio value. Lower drawdowns reflect better risk control and stability.
  • Expected Shortfall (ES_CVaR): Captures the expected loss under extreme market conditions. Lower values indicate better tail-risk management.

Key Takeaway: Effective risk management is indicated by low drawdowns and ES_CVaR, signaling the strategy can withstand adverse market conditions.

Risk-Adjusted Returns

Metrics measuring the returns adjusted for risk, such as Calmar Ratio, Sortino Ratio, Sharpe Ratio, Omega Ratio, and Sterling Ratio.
  • Calmar Ratio: The average annual compounded rate of return divided by maximum drawdown. Higher values indicate strong performance relative to risk.
  • Sortino Ratio: A variation of Sharpe Ratio focused on downside risk. Higher values suggest the strategy minimizes harmful volatility while generating returns.
  • Sharpe Ratio: Return of the strategy compared to its risk. A higher Sharpe Ratio reflects better risk-adjusted performance.
  • Omega Ratio: Likelihood of achieving a target return versus falling below it. Higher Omega Ratios suggest favorable risk-reward characteristics.
  • Sterling Ratio: The ratio of average annual return to maximum drawdown. Higher values indicate consistent performance with minimal risk.

Key Takeaway: Strong risk-adjusted metrics reflect a strategy capable of generating returns while controlling volatility and drawdowns effectively.

Performance Over Time

Metrics tracking the performance trends of the strategy over time, including CAGR, total score, and Gain-Loss Ratio.
  • Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR): Annualized returns over a specified period. Higher CAGR values indicate consistent long-term growth.
  • Total Score: Aggregate measure reflecting strategy performance across multiple metrics. Useful for overall assessment and ranking of strategies.
  • Gain-Loss Ratio: The average gain relative to the average loss, indicating the balance of positive and negative returns. Higher values suggest a favorable reward-to-risk profile.

Key Takeaway: Strong performance over time metrics suggest the strategy delivers consistent growth and balances gains against losses effectively.

Return versus Volatilty Plot

This chart shows the relationship between returns and volatility in a strategy over time. By plotting the rolling mean of cumulative returns against the rolling standard deviation (volatility), you can observe how returns are impacted by risk.
  • The X-axis represents volatility (rolling standard deviation). Higher values indicate more risk or unpredictability, while lower values suggest more stability.
  • The Y-axis shows the rolling mean of cumulative profits (returns). Higher values indicate greater returns, while lower values suggest smaller gains or even losses.
  • Each point on the chart reflects the return and volatility at a specific time. Clusters of points can indicate trends, such as high returns with low volatility (upper-left) or fluctuating relationships between risk and return.
Key Insights:
  • Return-to-Risk Ratio: Ideally, high returns with low volatility appear in the upper-left. Points further right imply higher risk for the shown returns.
  • Risk Efficiency: Trends showing increasing returns alongside rising volatility suggest the strategy takes on more risk as returns grow.
  • Risk Control: If points remain in the low-volatility range but returns increase, the strategy is effectively managing risk while achieving good returns.
  • Outliers: Points far from the main cluster may indicate unusual periods of high returns or volatility, worth examining for specific causes.

Risk versus Return Plot

This scatter plot visualizes the relationship between risk (drawdown) and return (profit) for each period. Each point represents a specific period's drawdown and corresponding profit.
  • The X-axis shows the drawdown percentage, where higher values indicate greater risk due to larger drops from peak profit. Lower values reflect more stable periods with smaller declines.
  • The Y-axis represents absolute profit (return), with positive values for profitable periods and negative values for losing periods.
  • Each point represents a period's risk (X-axis) and return (Y-axis), while the red trend line shows the overall relationship between risk and return. An upward slope suggests higher risk is associated with higher returns, while a downward slope signals inefficiency.
Key Insights:
  • Risk-Return Trade-off: A positive trend line indicates that higher risk tends to bring higher returns, while a negative or flat line suggests inefficiency in risk-taking.
  • Cluster of Points: Points near the origin indicate low risk and low return (stable but less profitable). Points in the upper right represent high risk and high return periods. Points in the lower right (high drawdown, low return) are undesirable.
  • Extreme Values: Outliers (points far from the main cluster) show periods of extreme profit or loss, offering insights into how the strategy handles extreme market conditions.
  • Strategy Efficiency: The ideal is a cluster in the upper left (low risk, high return), showing consistent returns with minimal drawdown. Points in the lower right suggest inefficiency and potential need for strategy adjustment.
